What Makes Our Coffee Speacial?

First, it's the plant itself. Specialty coffee is exclusively made of a family of varieties under the Arabica species. Coffea Arabica is a delicate plant that is prone to diseases and requires specific conditions to grow- rich soil and high altitudes.

A key part in why our coffee tastes different is undoubtedly the origin of the beans. Each growing region on the planet (and there are a few) helps give our coffee specific traits or characteristics that therefore enhance the flavour profile.

In conclusion, people love specialty coffee because it provides a unique and diverse flavor experience, as well as a level of care and attention that is unmatched by regular coffee. Specialty coffee is produced with ethical and sustainable practices, which benefits both the environment and the farmers who produce it.
